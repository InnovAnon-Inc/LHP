(By the Good Graces of Ganesha) The End has Come

I'd like to explore the seventh mood--the darkest mood--the unresolving mood. This piece is in C Locrian. (play scale) I'd like to really feel that diminished starting chord. A long, long time ago when I was little, I saw a vision of an old lady with the body of a man. I called her Muscle Grandma. Muscle Grandma was a superhero, but she didn't fight crime like normal superheroes. Normal heroes fight robbers each day, and each day begin again with the same work. At that age I didn't know the story of Sisyphus. Well anyway, Mucle Grandma was old and clever, so she'd fix the things that made the robbers want to steal. That way the other superheroes could take a break sometimes. And she was a granny, so she gave people cookies and cakes. One Samhain, "Halloween" to me at the time, I dressed as Muscle Grandma, and went to the festival at a church. I'd also like to tell you a story. This is a true story. The Big Bad Wolf begins by eating everyone. He proceeds to shit everywhere and on everything. I guess he's got some sort of shitting disorder. Anyway, The End inevitably comes, and a little old lady kicks his ass in self-defense and then he has to clean up all his shit and help fix the things he broke.
